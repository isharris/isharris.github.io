Motivation

Inertial measurement units (IMUs) are small sensor packs that include accelerometers, gyroscopes, and magnetometers which make them ideal for a wide variety of robotics applications including the measurement of joint angles. To determine absolute orientation and location of the object they are attached to, IMU signals must be integrated, so error in their output is vulnerable to drift during long periods use. Additional error can also be introduced through magnetic interference with the magnetometer readings. To combat this, calibration and post-processing algorithms can be made to adjust for these measurement errors, but ground truth angle data is needed to quantify their performance.

Contribution

Working with four other teammates, I developed a measurement apparatus that can collect ground truth angles for comparison with those derived from IMU algorithms. Throughout the entirety of the project, I led all aspects of the electrical and software design, which included sensor, battery, and microcontroller selection, assembly of our data acquisition unit, and firmware creation using C++.

Flow Diagram for Data Acquisition System

Results

During a period with limited access to manufacturing resources due to COVID 19 restrictions, my team delivered a fully functioning prototype to our stakeholder which met all key design requirements and specifications. The requirements achieved by my electrical design included:

1. Total cost less than $400

2. Measuring angles to within ± 0.2°

3. Reliable sampling at 300Hz

4. Capable of lasting over 8 hours on a single battery charge

5. Easy method for storing and transferring large quantities of data
