Mechatronic Ankle Showcase

Creative and Elegant Design

Motivation

The human ankle joint exhibits a varying stiffness during different tasks and phases of gait. Traditional prosthetic feet are unable to mimic this biological behavior which can lead to a poor experience for the amputee. To address this issue, researchers at the University of Michigan Neurobionics Lab developed the Variable Stiffness Ankle Prosthesis, or VSPA. To promote this novel technology, I worked with two other teammates to create an interactive display for the first VSPA prototype. More than just a pretty podium, this showcase also educates onlookers about the engineering concepts behind the VSPA foot.

Contribution

I had my hand in all aspects of the VSPA display which included everything from the wood working to the glowing acrylic sign on the front of the podium, but my primary contribution was integrating the VSPA electronics into a graphical user interface (GUI). The GUI was built using a Python state machine which would loop through various modes depending on user input via two LED buttons and feedback from the ankle's encoders.

Results

My team's hard work resulted in a beautiful showcase which can be found standing in the University of Michigan Orthotics and Prosthetics Center. Bug free and virtually autonomous, the demo has been running smoothly ever since its 2018 debut.
