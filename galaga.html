Training a Galaga Fighter with Reinforcement Learning

Background

PufferLib is an increasingly popular repository which provides a convenient framework for training reinforcement learning agents. Among its claims to fame is its extremely high throughput, allowing for training agents orders of magnitudes faster than traditional workflows. It achieves this by leveraging vectorized environments, direct memory access to contiguous observation buffers, and fast simulation environments written in C. Given its promising capability, I decided to fork the repo, and try my hand at training a Galaga fighter with it. Galaga is a classic arcade game that I have fond memories of playing with my Dad, and it consists of just 3 actions: left, right, and shoot - making it an easy example to test out. This first required creating a new sim environment (the Galaga game) and then a few hours of training, tweaking, and re-training. While the environment is admittedly very simple, and I am not doing any meaningful evaluation post-training, I was pleasantly surprised I was able to train a reasonably competent fighter without too much effort.

About

A Galaga-style arcade game environment built with PufferLib. The game is compiled to WebAssembly via Emscripten and rendered with raylib, allowing a trained RL agent to run entirely in the browser.
